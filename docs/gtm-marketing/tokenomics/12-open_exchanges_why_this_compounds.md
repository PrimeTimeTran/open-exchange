### Strategic Framing: The Long Game

We have reached the end of the beginning. We have built from first principles, designed for adversarial markets, verified our systems publicly, tested with real capital, and admitted our failures. Now, we look at the horizon.

The defining characteristic of successful financial infrastructure is "Inevitability." It does not win by being the loudest; it wins by being the most reliable. Trust compounds. Every day the exchange operates without failure, its value increases.

### Technical & Numerical Reality

**The Flywheel of Resilience:**

1.  **Safety First:** We allocated capital to audits and liquidity, not ads.
2.  **Trust Earned:** Users see verifiable solvency and fair execution.
3.  **Volume Grows:** Organic volume arrives because the product works.
4.  **Revenue Compounds:** Fees are reinvested into the Insurance Fund and deeper liquidity.
5.  **Moat Widens:** The system becomes harder to attack and better to use.

We track "Trust Metrics" (Time Uptime, Total Settled Volume, Days Since Incident) as our primary KPIs.
